How the one-rep max is calculated

A one-rep max formula predicts the heaviest weight you could lift for a single rep from a lighter set taken closer to failure. This tool averages three well-established formulas:

  • Epley: weight × (1 + reps ÷ 30)
  • Brzycki: weight × 36 ÷ (37 − reps)
  • Lombardi: weight × reps0.10

Estimates are most accurate for sets of about 10 reps or fewer taken close to failure. The further you get from a true max — very high reps, or lots of reps left in the tank — the rougher the estimate. Treat the number as a training guide, not a guarantee.
