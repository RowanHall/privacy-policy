Best free workout tracker apps for lifters (2026)

Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

There are dozens of workout trackers, and most of the "free" ones bury the useful parts behind a subscription. If you lift weights and want to actually track progress — not just check a box — a few things separate a great tracker from a frustrating one. Here's what to look for, and the app we'd put at the top for lifters.

What makes a workout tracker worth using

Every good lifting tracker earns its place on these criteria:

  • Fast logging. You're between sets, not sitting at a desk. Entering a set should take a couple of taps, not a form.
  • Automatic PRs and estimated 1RM. The app should spot personal records and track your estimated one-rep max so you don't do math on the gym floor.
  • Real progress stats. Volume trends, per-exercise performance, and lifetime totals — the numbers that tell you whether the program is working.
  • Consistency tools. Streaks and a history you can actually browse keep you showing up.
  • Genuinely free, no ads. The core logging experience shouldn't be paywalled or interrupted.
  • Your data is yours. Secure sync, easy export, and instant account deletion.
