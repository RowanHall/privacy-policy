Workout tracker app vs spreadsheet

Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

Plenty of strong lifters track training in a spreadsheet, and it works. It's free, endlessly customizable, and yours forever. But spreadsheets also have real friction — especially on your phone, mid-workout. Here's an honest look at both, so you can pick what fits.

The spreadsheet: pros and cons

A spreadsheet is a blank canvas. That's its biggest strength and its biggest weakness.

  • Free and flexible. Build any layout, formula, or chart you want.
  • Full control of your data. It's a file you own outright.
  • Clumsy on a phone. Tiny cells and fiddly typing between sets get old fast.
  • Manual everything. PRs, estimated 1RM, and volume trends only exist if you build and maintain the formulas.
  • Easy to abandon. A little friction each session adds up, and many spreadsheets quietly die after a few weeks.

A dedicated app: pros and cons

  • Built for the gym floor. Set logging is a couple of taps, with a rest timer built in.
  • Automatic analysis. PRs, estimated 1RM, volume and muscle-split stats are calculated for you.
  • Consistency built in. Streaks, history and reminders keep you showing up.
  • Less customizable. You work within the app's structure rather than inventing your own.
  • Check data portability. A good app lets you export your history and delete your account easily — make sure yours does.

Which should you use?

If you love tinkering with formulas and run a very specific programming method, a spreadsheet can be perfect. But if you just want to log fast, see your progress, and never miss a PR, a dedicated tracker wins on every rep of the session — because the analysis happens automatically.

FAQ

Is a spreadsheet good for tracking workouts?

Yes, for lifters who want total control and don't mind manual setup and slower phone entry. The trade-off is friction and the need to build your own stats.

Can I move my spreadsheet data into an app?

It depends on the app. Some let you import a CSV; others you'll simply start fresh. Good apps also let you export back out, so you're never locked in.
